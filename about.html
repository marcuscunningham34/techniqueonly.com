Where this came from

Twenty-six years in the Air Force. C-17s and KC-46s. Deployments, command, staff work, and a body that quietly accumulated the operational cost of all of it. OSA. A 30-pound weight problem that was actually a metabolic problem that was actually a sleep problem. A VA rating at 100% that wasn't a destination — it was a set of constraints to work within.

Retirement wasn't a finish line. It was a restructuring of the problem. The question that organized everything after separation: what physical and professional capability do I owe the people who depend on me?

The autistic son. The daughters. The work that continues at RAND. The question has a concrete answer and the answer requires method.

The operational mission

Maintain physical and cognitive capability to care for a dependent child with complex needs over a decades-long time horizon. This is not a wellness goal. It is a logistics problem with human stakes and a long planning window.

Every LOE in this company connects back to this. Human Optimization is the primary operation. Mead, beekeeping, aviation, 3D printing, and poultry are secondary — but they share the same methodology and they all generate documentation. The documentation is the discipline. The discipline is the mission.

The philosophy — Technique as method

The name came before the LLC. Technique as a noun rather than a descriptor. Not "good technique" or "proper technique" — just technique: a process, a discipline, a methodology. Applied identically across every domain without regard to outcome.

You can control the method. You cannot control the result. The method is therefore the only honest thing to document.

"The Buddha, the Godhead, resides quite as comfortably in the circuits of a digital computer or the gears of a cycle transmission as he does at the top of a mountain or in the petals of a flower. To think otherwise is to demean the Buddha — which is to demean oneself."
Robert Pirsig — Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance

This book is the canonical reference not because of what it says about motorcycles but because of what it demonstrates about quality as a practice. The narrator is not teaching you to maintain a motorcycle. He is showing you what happens when someone cares enough about a thing to understand it completely. That is the model.

The 20-year training log is not a record of results. The Bod Pod scan series is not a progress photo. The fermentation data is not a recipe collection. These are records of a practice. The practice is what's for sale — not the outcomes, which vary, and not the prescriptions, which don't generalize.

The voice — non-negotiable

This is where most people in this space fail. They acquire data and then prescribe. The prescription is where they lose the thread, because a prescription requires that what worked for one person in one context generalizes to another person in another context. It does not. The data does not support this.

The only honest thing to say is what happened. Not what should happen. Not what will happen. What happened, when, and under what conditions.

The rule in practice

Never

  • "Here's what you should do."
  • "This works for everyone."
  • "Try this."
  • "The best approach is…"

Always

  • "Here's what I observed."
  • "Here's what the data showed."
  • "Here's where I got it wrong."
  • "Here's what I changed and why."

The distinction matters because the reader is not the author. The author is 47, male, 100% VA disabled, with a specific set of constraints, a specific body, a specific metabolic history. The reader probably isn't. The data is transferable. The prescription is not.

Who is reading this

The primary reader is a veteran 45–60 years old navigating the gap between service identity and what comes after it. The transition question is usually framed professionally — what do I do next? — but the real question is operational: what does my body owe the people who need it, and for how long, and at what standard?

The secondary reader is anyone exhausted by prescriptive content who wants to see an honest account of a documented practice. The methodology doesn't belong to the military context. It belongs to anyone who has tried to do something rigorously and found that the internet gives them marketing copy instead of field notes.

If this is you: the data is here. The method is documented. The conclusions are provisional. The practice continues.
